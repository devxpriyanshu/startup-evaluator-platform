Method logic

Primary recommendation (CRS-driven, with TRL as secondary guard):

  • CRS < 50 → Berkus (early, limited commercial signal)
  • CRS 50–69 → Scorecard
  • CRS 70–84 & TRL ≥ 5 → VC Method
  • CRS ≥ 85 & TRL ≥ 7 → Risk-adjusted DCF

Stage-to-method reference — the CRS rule above is calibrated against these canonical stage/method pairings:

StageTypical TRLPreferred method(s)
Idea stage1–3Berkus (primary) · Scorecard (if sector comparables exist)
Prototype4–5Scorecard
Pilot6–7VC Method (Scorecard as cross-check)
Early revenue7–8Revenue Multiple (current ARR) · Peer Multiple
Growth8–9Revenue Multiple (current or Y3 ARR) · Peer Multiple
Mature9Risk-adjusted DCF (primary) · Peer Multiple (triangulation)

Why Revenue Multiple is the most common startup lens. Startups typically have unstable profits, negative cash flow, and unpredictable growth — conditions that make DCF's terminal value and WACC assumptions speculative. Revenue is observable and listed-peer multiples are available, so Revenue Multiple (and Peer Multiple) are the default for early-revenue through growth stages. DCF becomes the primary lens only once cash flows are proven and repeatable (mature stage).

Projection basis for Revenue Multiple. Choose in the card above:

  • Current ARR (trailing) — use for mature, revenue-generating companies (TRL ≥ 8 with 5+ years of revenue). Multiplying current ARR by a sector multiple mirrors how listed comparables are priced.
  • Year 3 ARR — growth-stage companies where current revenue is small but near-term ramp is credible (Series A/B).
  • Year 7 ARR — long-horizon VC lens; use when forward hyper-growth is the thesis and a credible plan exists.
